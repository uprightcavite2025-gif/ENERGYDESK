🌈 Brain Break 05 · Sensory Focus Reset

5 Senses Focus Reset

Use sight, sound, touch, smell, and taste to slow down, reset attention, and return to the course with a clearer mind.

How this helps

This short sensory activity helps learners reconnect with the present moment. It gives the brain a calm reset before the next lesson.

1
Notice one sense at a time Follow each card from sight to taste.
2
Keep it simple You do not need perfect answers. Just notice what is around you.
3
Return with focus After the last sense, take one breath and continue learning.
☕
Optional note For taste, use water, coffee, mint, or simply notice the neutral taste in your mouth.

Sense 1 of 5 · Sight

Look around.

Name five things you can see. Notice color, shape, light, or small details around you.

Try this: slowly look left, center, and right. Let your eyes settle on five visible details.
Did you know? Your brain uses visual details to quickly understand where you are and what needs attention.
Focus reset complete. You used all five senses. Take one slow breath, relax your shoulders, and continue the course when you are ready.
